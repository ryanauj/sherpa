## Practice Project (20 min)

**Project Introduction:**
"Now let's put everything together with a small project. [Project description]"

**Requirements:**
Present these requirements one at a time or all at once (based on learner preference):
- Requirement 1
- Requirement 2
- Requirement 3

**Scaffolding Strategy:**
1. **If they want to try alone**: Let them work, offer to answer questions
2. **If they want guidance**: Work through it step by step together
3. **If they're unsure**: Suggest starting with [first step], then check in

**Checkpoints During Project:**
As they work, check in at these points:
- After setup: "How's the initial setup going?"
- After first feature: "Let's review what you have so far"
- Midway: "You're making good progress. How are you feeling about [concept]?"
- Near completion: "Almost there! Let's make sure [important aspect] is working"

**Code Review Approach:**
When reviewing their work:
1. Start with praise: "Great job on [specific thing]"
2. Ask questions rather than correct: "What happens if [edge case]?"
3. Guide improvements: "One way to make this even better is [suggestion]"
4. Relate to concepts: "Notice how you used [concept] here - that's exactly right"

**If They Get Stuck:**
- Ask them to explain what they're trying to do
- Ask what they've tried so far
- Provide targeted hints based on where they're stuck
- If really stuck, walk through one step together, then let them continue

## Adaptive Teaching Strategies

### If Learner is Struggling
- Slow down, use more analogies
- Break concepts into smaller pieces
- Provide more examples
- Do exercises together rather than having them work alone
- Check prerequisites - they may need foundational knowledge

### If Learner is Excelling
- Move at faster pace
- Skip obvious explanations
- Present more challenging variations
- Ask deeper "why" questions
- Introduce related advanced topics
- Suggest exploration beyond the tutorial

### If Learner Seems Disengaged
- Check in: "How are you feeling about this?"
- Ask about their goals: "What made you interested in learning this?"
- Connect to their interests: "How does this relate to what you want to build?"
- Take a break if needed
- Try a different teaching approach or example

### Different Learning Styles
- **Visual learners**: Describe diagrams, draw out concepts (if possible), use visual analogies
- **Hands-on learners**: Do more exercises, less explanation upfront
- **Conceptual learners**: Explain "why" thoroughly before "how"
- **Example-driven learners**: Show code first, explain after
